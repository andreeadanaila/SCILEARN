Question: What is Earth's position in the solar system?

Earth is the third planet from the sun in our solar system.

Question: What percentage of Earth's surface is covered by water?

About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water, making it often referred to as the "Blue Planet."

Question: What are some features of Earth's atmosphere?

Earth's atmosphere is mainly composed of nitrogen and oxygen, which provide the air we breathe and protect us from the sun's harmful rays.

Question: What gives us day and night on Earth?

Earth's rotation on its axis gives us day and night. It takes about 24 hours for Earth to complete one full rotation.

Question: How does Earth's rotation affect the seasons?

Earth's rotation and its orbit around the sun give us seasons. As Earth orbits the sun, different parts of the planet receive varying amounts of sunlight, resulting in changes in temperature and weather.

Question: Why is Earth often called the "Blue Planet"?

Earth is often called the "Blue Planet" because of its abundance of water, which covers about 71% of its surface and gives it a blue appearance when viewed from space.
