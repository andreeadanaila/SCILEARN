Discovering Mercury

❮ ❯


Question: What is Mercury?

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest one to the sun. It's like a tiny, rocky world orbiting around the sun.

Question: What does Mercury look like?

Mercury has a grayish-brown, rocky surface covered in craters. It looks dry and barren, with no plants or oceans like Earth.

Question: What is unique about Mercury's temperature?

Mercury experiences extreme temperatures. During the day, it can get scorching hot, reaching up to about 800 degrees Fahrenheit (430 degrees Celsius). At night, it gets very cold because it lacks an atmosphere to hold onto the heat.

Question: How long is a day on Mercury?

One day on Mercury, which is the time it takes for it to rotate once on its axis, is about 59 Earth days.

Question: What is unique about Mercury's rotation?

Mercury spins slowly compared to Earth, but it orbits the sun much faster. Its year, the time it takes to orbit around the sun, is only about 88 Earth days.

Question: How do scientists study Mercury?

Scientists study Mercury using spacecraft like MESSENGER, which took pictures and measurements to help us learn more about its surface, atmosphere, and magnetic field.
