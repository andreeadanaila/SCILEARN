Discovering Venus

❮ ❯


Question: What is Venus often called, and why?

Venus is often called the "morning star" or the "evening star" because it's one of the brightest objects in the sky. You can see it shining brightly just before sunrise or just after sunset.

Question: What makes Venus so hot?

Venus is incredibly hot due to a runaway greenhouse effect caused by its thick atmosphere, which traps heat from the sun. It's even hotter than Mercury, the closest planet to the sun!

Question: How does Venus' rotation differ from most other planets?

Venus spins very slowly, and it spins in the opposite direction compared to most other planets. This means the sun rises in the west and sets in the east on Venus, which is the opposite of what we see on Earth!

Question: What is Venus' atmosphere made of?

Venus has a thick atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid. These clouds reflect sunlight and contribute to Venus' extreme heat.

Question: How have scientists explored Venus?

Scientists have sent several spacecraft to explore Venus and study its mysterious atmosphere and surface. These spacecraft have used special instruments to peer through the thick clouds and map Venus' surface, revealing volcanoes, mountains, and other geological features.

Question: Why is Venus called Earth's sister planet?

Venus is called Earth's sister planet because they are similar in size and composition. They are both rocky planets, but Venus' extreme heat and thick atmosphere make it very different from Earth.
