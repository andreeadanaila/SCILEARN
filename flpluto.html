Questions: What is Pluto made of?

Pluto is made up of a mantle of ice (mostly Nitrogen ice) and a rocky core, each making up about 50% of its mass.

Question: How is Pluto's orbit different from the other planets?

Pluto's orbit is egg-shaped, not round like the 8 planets. It can be as close as 2.8 billion miles from the sun and as far as 5 billion miles away.

Question: What happens to Pluto's atmosphere as it moves away from the sun?

When Pluto moves away from the sun, it gets so cold that its thin atmosphere starts to freeze and fall to the ground.

Question: How does Pluto compare to Earth?

Pluto has a hard, rocky surface like Earth, but it is much smaller and extremely cold because it is so far away from the sun.

Question: How did we first discover Pluto?

Pluto was discovered in 1930 by a young astronomer named Clyde Tombaugh after a year of searching. He was looking for a 9th planet beyond Neptune, called Planet X.

Question: How do we know more about Pluto today?

We know more about Pluto today thanks to the New Horizons space probe, which flew past Pluto in 2015. It came within 7,800 miles of Pluto's surface, taking pictures and mapping the chemical compositions of Pluto and its moon Charon.
