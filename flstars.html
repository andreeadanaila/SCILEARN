Question: What is a galaxy?

A galaxy is a group of stars and other space stuff. They can have trillions of stars and spin around a center of high gravity.

Question: How many galaxies are there in the universe?

Scientists think there are over 100 billion galaxies in the universe.

Question: What is the Milky Way?

The Milky Way is the galaxy we live in. It's a spiral-shaped galaxy with around 300 billion stars.

Question: What are the four main types of galaxies?

The four main types are Spiral, Barred Spiral, Elliptical, and Irregular.

Question: What are the characteristics of a spiral galaxy?

Spiral galaxies have long arms spiraling around the center, with older stars in the center and newer stars in the arms.

Question: What are some fun facts about galaxies?

Galaxies are named after the Greek word for "milky," most of their mass might be made up of dark matter, they likely have massive black holes in their centers, and Andromeda is the closest galaxy to the Milky Way.
