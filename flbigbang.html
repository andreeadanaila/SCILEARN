Question: What is the Big Bang?

The Big Bang is when the universe started from a tiny dot that exploded, creating everything we see today—stars, planets, and even us. It's like a giant fireworks show that happened a very long time ago.

Question: What happened to the universe after the Big Bang?

After the Big Bang, the universe started to cool and expand. Particles formed atoms of hydrogen and helium, which later formed stars and planets. The universe is still cooling and expanding today.

Question: When did the Big Bang occur?

Most astronomers believe the Big Bang happened about 14 billion years ago, starting from a bubble smaller than a pinhead.

Question: Who came up with the theory of the Big Bang?

Georges Lemaître, a Belgian priest, mathematician, and astronomer, first thought of the idea in 1927.

Question: What is cosmology?

Cosmology is the study of the universe, including its creation and development.

Question: When were the earliest stars and galaxies created?

The earliest whole stars and galaxies were created around 560 million years after the Big Bang.
